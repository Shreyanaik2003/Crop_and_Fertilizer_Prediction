Organic Vegetables

Organic vegetables are grown without synthetic pesticides, fertilizers, GMOs, or ionizing radiation, relying instead on natural pest control and soil fertility methods. They must meet specific certification standards and are considered more environmentally friendly. Though often more expensive, they are believed by some to be healthier and tastier. Organic farming promotes sustainability and biodiversity. The nutritional differences between organic and conventional vegetables remain debated.

Organic Fruits

Organic fruits are cultivated without synthetic pesticides, fertilizers, or GMOs, using natural pest management and soil enhancement techniques. They must adhere to strict certification standards to be labeled organic. Organic farming aims to be environmentally friendly, promoting sustainability and biodiversity. These fruits are often pricier due to labor-intensive practices and lower yields. While some believe organic fruits are healthier and taste better, the nutritional differences compared to conventional fruits are still debated.

“Alone we can do little; together we can do so much”. With this as their belief, four smallholder farmers led by Ms Siri took their first steps on a long and purposeful journey. Since its inception, Green Valley remains fully committed to helping farmers overcome the barriers to finance, technology adoption and product value addition. ‍ In time, Green Valley's self-contained infrastructure encompassed an agro-advisory team, a world-class pack-house, cold storage facility and a technology backbone to help achieve scale meaningfully. Soon enough, Green Valley Farms became a hub of value addition by building and operating an agri-value chain that was efficient, expedient and innovative.
